# Syllabus

## Course Info
- **Duration:** 10 weeks  
- **Format:** 2h lecture + 1h exercise per week  
- **Audience:** 18+, basic math, retail investors / aspiring quants  

## Learning Outcomes
By the end, students will be able to:
- Understand time value of money, markets, and instruments
- Read financial statements and interpret key ratios
- Apply basic quantitative tools (returns, volatility, Sharpe)
- Build and evaluate a personal investment plan
- (Optional) Design and backtest simple strategies

## Weekly Schedule
| Week | Topic                                  | Key Concepts                         |
|------|----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| 1    | Money, Time, and Markets              | PV/FV, stocks, bonds, ETFs          |
| 2    | Financial Statements & Ratios          | Balance sheet, P/E, ROE, D/E        |
| 3    | Risk, Return, and Diversification     | Volatility, diversification, allocation |
| 4    | Quantitative Basics: Returns & Volatility | Returns, standard deviation, Sharpe |
| 5    | Valuation Basics                      | DCF, dividend discount, multiples   |
| 6    | Portfolio Theory Basics               | Portfolio risk, CAPM, beta         |
| 7    | Risk Management & Behavioral Finance  | Drawdowns, biases, position sizing  |
| 8    | Data & Tools (Light Intro)            | Data sources, spreadsheets, Python/R |
| 9    | Simple Quantitative Strategies         | Momentum, mean reversion, backtesting |
| 10   | Capstone: Personal Investor Plan      | Synthesis, ethics, next steps       |

## Assessment
- Weekly quizzes: 20%
- Problem sets & small reports: 40%
- Final personal investment plan: 40%

## Prerequisites
- Basic algebra and statistics
- Access to a spreadsheet (Excel/Google Sheets)
- Optional: Python/R for advanced exercises
